Hi! I'm a forex trader intermade.

So, forex trading is basically like exchanging money, but with the goal of making a little extra cash. Imagine you have some pula, and you think the dollar is going to go up in value. You trade your pula for dollars now, and later, when the dollar gets “stronger,” you swap it back. That difference? That’s your profit.

1.How It Works


Currencies are always in pairs. You’re not just buying a dollar—you’re buying it against something else.
For example:
(i).EUR/USD → Euro vs US Dollar
(ii).GBP/USD → British Pound vs US Dollar

Think of it like betting on which one will get stronger. You buy if you think the first currency will rise, and sell if you think it will fall.
It’s all about timing. Buy when it’s low, sell when it’s high. Sounds simple, but prices can jump up and down a lot, sometimes in minutes. That’s why traders watch charts and news all the time.

3.Facts and Myths about trading

#You can see live prices, graphs, and place trades with a click. It’s like a video game but with real money on the line.
#It’s open 24/7 you can trade any time.
#You can start with a small amount of cash.
#The market is huge, so opportunities are everywhere.
#You can lose money fast
#If you let emotions take over, you’ll make mistakes
#Using too much “borrowed money” can wipe out your account
#What Helps You Win
#Learn to read charts (technical analysis)
#Pay attention to world news (fundamental analysis)
#Don’t risk more than you can afford
#Stay calm and patient


In short: Forex trading isn’t a quick rich scheme. It’s more like a skill you learn, practice, and slowly get better. Some people love it; some people get burned if they rush.
